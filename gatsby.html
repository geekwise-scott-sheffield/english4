The Great Gatsby

Great Gatsby - Characters
Name Age Description / Style Economic Situation Trait / Mentality Motivations Motivation Commonalities
Nick Carraway ~late 20's Typical Mid-Western turned big city yuppie. What would be called a wallflower. Born to a middle class family, now in persuit of wealth through the bond business. Shy in comparison to the other characters. Always takes the time to analyze a situation. Desires the friendship of Gatsby. Typically a passive "yes man" from beginning to end. Like F. Scott Fitzgerald, who's an outcast desiring (and at the same time resents) to be let in
Jay Gatsby ~late 20's Very well dressed and proper. Always optimistic yet modest with others New money, lived in a mansion on West Egg Insecure. Tries to compensate this with physical things and objects He wants Daisy. Everything he's done is to try to get Daisy Motivations are similar to F. Scott Fitzgerald
Daisy Buchanan ~late 20's cynical in her interactions, but appears to be very genuine and trustworthy. Became wealthy by marrying Tom Buchanan. Hides true emotions whenever possible. Cynical. Money Oriented. Similar to Zelda.
Tom Buchanan ~early 30's Buff, tall, very into sports, a mans man. Born rich or "old money". Arrogant, very agressive and likes to be dominant. Power. The more he posses, the safer he feels. Unique. What F. Scott Fitzgerald desired to be.
Jordan Baker ~early to mid 20's Tall and skinny, athletic. Beautiful face. Wealthy friends of the Buchanan's Dishonest, and romantic. "win" she applied life to an athletic mindset to "win" and get ahead. Amanthis Powell is somewhat similar
Myrtle Wilson ~mid 30's Thick-ish, somewhat small. carried her extra weight beautifully Middle Class Jealous by nature. She desired the attention and wealth of Tom Somewhat similar to Zelda.
George Wilson ~mid 30's Blonde, seemingly emotionless. Mildly handsome. Middle Class Jealous yet loving. Seeks approval of Myrtle Similar to F. Scott Fitzgerald, with Zelda.
